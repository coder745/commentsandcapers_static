How my Stories Came About

About six years ago when a friend, who had his own daily devotion website, challenged me to try to write a devotion/story, I was really surprised. I love to write letters but I had never written anything of that nature before. After much encouragement, I said I would try. Twenty nine devotions later I am thrilled with the way God has used my love of writing to bless others.. God has given me the thoughts and any success along this line is because of His guidance and leading for which I praise and thank Him.

I especially want to share God's leading as He teaches me the joys of dependence on his Grace and Mercy in all things through His Word. It is only through the love and grace of God and encouragement of my friends and family that I have been able to put my thoughts on paper. I can see his hand of guidance in all the ups and downs of my life. Believe me there have been plenty of both. He has never once failed me and today my desire is to please him and to encourage others to live for Him.

My life growing up in the years of the Great Depression were stressful and uncertain. We had a large family the going was tough. We learned to get by with the simple things and that with all of the joys and sorrows that come into our lives comes the realization that God was there all the time teaching us and working out his will in our lives for our good.. My desire to share has been a major part of my love of writing.

"All things work together for Good to them that love God…" Everything that comes our way teaches us that our actions and reactions will affect others. My stories will reflect on experiences that happened then and more recently and how I have learned from them. The illustrations I have used from God's word bring home the truth that nothing just happens. God planned it all. I am thankful to Him for all of it.

I firmly believe that God leads us in every area of our lives as we seek his will and desire to serve him. I hope you will enjoy reading these pages as much as I have enjoyed writing them.
