Good & Evil

The beautiful story of Esther in the Bible involves more than a romance. It is about how God moves and is about the continuing battle between good and evil. The story begins in the book of Genesis and reaches its climax in this book. One of the final links in the chain of events that make up the story of God’s plan to bring the Messiah, Jesus, into the world through the Jewish race.

The battle is God’s but he uses people. Mordecai was Esther’s adoptive father. He took her in after the death of her parents. He was a caring, kind man. He was a Eunuch, a servant and a member of a captive race of people. Whatever his job, in this country of his captivity, he did it knowing the law of God and his responsibility to it.

Mordecai, like us, had to deal with people who were thoughtless and unkind. As a known personality in the palace, he did his job well. He also had human fear but did not hide from reality. When an evil man took the place of authority in the palace, he caused grief and sorrow everywhere. At the risk of his life Mordecai refused to honor mans law over God’s. The apostle Peter also said, “We ought to obey God rather than man. For anyone too go against God just to please man is not very wise.

God gave Mordecai and Esther courage in the face of danger. He said in 2 Chronicles 20:15… “The battle is the not yours, but God’s.” He will do the same for us if we give him first place in our lives. God cared about the fate of Esther and Mordecai and He cares about you, too.

God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble.(Psalm46) Why not give Him a try?
