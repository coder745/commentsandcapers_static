Homesick

2 Corinthians 3:5

I was fearful as I looked into the eyes of the other students on my first day of school away from home. They were all strangers moving into the dorm and very busy putting their things in place. Everyone seemed happy to meet new people. They were all making friends easily and looking forward to a great school year. However for me, a quiet and shy self-conscious introvert, it was a feeling of what am I doing here? Tears filled my eyes and I wanted to just go home and forget this place. I knew no one and it was hard for me to make friends.

My first days are a blur because I was so homesick. It had been a big step to commit myself to the Lord’s service when I was too shy to even talk to people. How would I ever make it? I was afraid to have a roommate because I would not know how to talk to her or to relate to others in our section of the dorm.

Life is tough enough in the world even for people with self-assurance and confidence that they can do anything. I was sure I could do nothing and might even be a failure at that. Classes were underway and as I look back I can see the way the Lord had a plan and a purpose in it all.

I stood before the public speaking class and trembled. In order to pass the course, I had to make a speech. The only thing I could think of was a scary story. I acted the part without even trying. I scared the class with the story and I was petrified by the looks in all those eyes watching me. My voice trembled and my knees knocked. The teacher thought I did great because I was so realistic. The students thought I was a jerk for scaring them half to death when my story ended as a joke.

Through this experience I saw the light. I began to realize that to serve God is not to rely on my talent or ability; it is in being yielded to Him. God was working out the kinks in the life of this bashful person who wanted with all her heart to serve Him. That day was the beginning of change in my life.

I learned that when I thought of myself as nothing then I could allow the Lord to work out his plan for my life. He taught me to rely on Him and to let him work in me and through me. He has never failed me. Homesick? Not any more. Now home is anywhere I can serve Him.

“Not that we are sufficient of ourselves to think anything as of ourselves, but our sufficiency is of God.”
