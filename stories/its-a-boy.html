It’s a Boy

Life has never been the same since a little boy opened his eyes to a big New World that was his to conquer. His sweet smile and healthy lungs let the rest of the world know that he had arrived. Soon everyone around knew things were about to change.

I believe science and technology are born in the mechanism that makes up a little boy’s body and brain. At least the beginnings are there. His seeming abilities are boundless and his determination endless. He has no fear of heights and cares not that his incessant desire to climb may put him in harms way. He will attempt to climb anything that is climbable.

He is mischievous and messy, confident and fearless. He can build and demolish in record time. His feeling of satisfaction is not in the looks of an item but in finding out how it is put together. What better way than to take it apart.

Nothing is safe anymore. Pounding on everything to show his strength is important to him. He thinks he can out do anyone twice his size. He is dynamite in his ability to make things disappear…now you see it, now you don’t.

When caught in his mischief, the look in his eyes and his cry of anguish, be it ever so false, will melt your anger into pity and he has you like putty in his hands. I’m sorry mama, he wails and then after a hug of forgiveness he smiles, satisfied that he has escaped the spanking he so richly deserved. Give him time and he will drive you crazy, exhaust your patience and steal your heart all at the same time.

We can learn from a little boy’s enthusiasm for life. He could teach us great lessons of courage, faith and trust. These would be valuable lessons in daring to do things that make a difference, even when they seem to be impossible. Lessons in saying I’m sorry and in thankfulness to God for forgiveness when we do not get the punishment that our sinfulness deserves.

Somehow a little boy knows his parent’s love is what makes him secure no matter what he does. We can rest in the security of knowing that God’s love is unconditional and that he will always forgive. We need to be like putty in His hands so that he can mold us according to his plan.

Thank God for little boys.
