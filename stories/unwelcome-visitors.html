Unwelcome Visitors

Have you ever had an uninvited visitor come to your church? One Sunday morning we learned that a group of them had slipped in unnoticed and were hiding in one of the restrooms. These were the kind of visitors who had no respect for God or man. They were the kind that slip in quietly and before you know it have, by unscrupulous tactics, caused havoc in the church. Their desire was to be fed. Not spiritual food just the kind that satisfies physical hunger. They came only to eat and eat they did in a very impolite way.

We began to realize that our church was in danger of crumbling. It was up to the church body to send a clear message to them to leave. We rose to the occasion and took the steps necessary to remove them. We called for outside help. This was not a matter of prayer it was a matter of action. We took that action by taking up a collection and calling the exterminator. Our message, “Termites, you are not welcome here and will have to leave”. You have heard about the importance of building up the church. Their plan was to eat it down.

Finding termites in your church is about like finding weevils in your food cupboard. They will ruin everything they touch. They, like termites, are very unwelcome visitors; little things that have a way of spoiling big things.

There are little things that can slip quietly into our lives and turn our normal routine of life into a disaster. They are the little sins that slowly but surely grow and take control of our lives leading us to Spiritual weakness. They eat away at the moral fiber of our lives causing our relationship to God and our testimony to crumble. They hinder the fruit of the Spirit from being effective in our lives.

Soon Envy, complaining and unrest replace love, joy, and peace. Longsuffering is out of the question and gentleness? Forget it! Eventually kindness is replaced by pettiness and Faith gives way to doubt. Meekness is overruled by temper (As Moses, when he struck the rock) Temperance is lost in the maze of selfishness and greed.

We can win against this unwelcome intruder, called sin, by the Grace of God and the cleansing power of the blood of our Lord Jesus Christ.

I John 1:9 “If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just o forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.”

When the termites of sin invade our lives we have two choices. We can welcome them or we can rise to the occasion and “nip them in the bud” by claiming victory in our Savior.

I choose Victory in Christ! It “works for me”.
