The Artist

I have the bighead today. It is not the self-conceited type or the unloving and uncaring type. It is because I have had a new portrait made of myself and I am so proud of it. In the portrait my head is very large with eyes that are closer together than normal. In fact they are touching each other. My nose is not very shapely but my mouth is smiling. I wonder what is going on in the mind of the artist. I have two large ears and very curly hair. I am smiling and looking to my right as though I am seeing something special. What do I see? I see my three-year-old granddaughter who is drawing this picture of me.

I have no body. I have arms that are coming-out from my cheeks and legs that are neatly fastened to my chin. My feet would possibly be a size twelve. Just to the right of my big head is a beautiful little head. The two arms sticking out of it’s left cheek are reaching toward me.

The words from the artist are what makes it all so beautiful as she says, “Memaw, (meaning grandmother) this is a picture of you and this little one is me. I am right here beside of you. “I love the picture because I love her”. The beauty of a picture is in the one who made it.

The beauty in the life of a Christian is in knowing Jesus Christ, the one who is the giver of life and knowing that he is always right there beside us.

Sometimes when life’s picture seems distorted and nothing seems to go right, we just need to look to Jesus. He wants to shape us and mold us according to His plan. He knows all about us. Psalms 139.

He is a caring and loving God. We can trust Him. He is in control of the paint and the brush. A picture is more beautiful when you know and love the artist.
