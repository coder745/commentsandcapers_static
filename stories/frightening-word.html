Frightening Word

One day I was sitting in the Hospital waiting room looking at the expressions on the faces of the many people there. There were looks of pain and anguish and looks of concern from family members who had brought them there. They were all waiting their turn to see someone who could help them. My reason for being there was the same as theirs. I, too, had been diagnosed with Cancer.

Cancer is a very frightening word that strikes fear into the heart of the one who has been summoned to the doctor’s office with instructions to “bring the family”. Even his efforts to soften the blow by explaining the new treatment techniques, did not change the feeling of panic. It goes without saying that my outlook on life changed that day.

One month after a series of twenty eight radiation treatments, I returned to the hospital for my final examination and progress report. Now was the real test of the faith for which I had prayed before the treatments. I have quoted Romans 8:28 to other people many times but now I needed to believe it myself. It is always easy to tell others what God says in Philippians about not to worry about anything but to pray and God will give peace. I had peace in my heart (mingled with a little human anxiety.).

The blessing, as God answered my prayers and those of my friends and family that day was to hear the doctor say, “you are well healed and I see no sign of any cancer”. I do not remember my exact words in the joy of that moment but I gave credit to the Lord and told the doctor and nurse of the many people who were praying for me. My doctor is a believer in miracles and I know that he would agree that this was, “the Lord’s doing and it is marvelous in our eyes”. Psalm 118:23.

As I drove home that day I thought of the words, “well healed”. I laughed as I thought, that sounds exactly like well heeled which has an altogether different meaning. One means to be well physically and the other means to be well off or wealthy. In my case I am both well and wealthy. Well in body and wealthy in blessings. Wealthy in knowing Jesus Christ as my Savior, Wealthy in knowing the “God that heals”, and in having friends and family who pray and believe.

I will treasure the experience because I learned the tremendous truth of the verse I had given ohters. “All things work together for good to them that love God…” If this had not happened I would have missed some of the greatest blessings in my life. Blessings of new friends for whom I can pray and opportunities to witness that are just beginning.

Never question the way God works. He is all wise and knows exactly what He is doing.
