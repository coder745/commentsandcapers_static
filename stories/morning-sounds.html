Morning Sounds

Proverbs 3:5-6

I was sitting on my deck very early one morning just listening to the sounds of a new day getting underway. Birds were singing and scurrying about looking for food. There was a breeze gently moving the leaves of my pecan tree where a squirrel was checking out the status of the future crop of nuts.

Then I saw a very interesting thing happening. A very large black ant was running across the deck floor. He was in a big hurry to get somewhere, although he didn’t seem to know where or exactly how to get there. He was just running. There are spaces between the boards on the deck big enough for him to fall through but on he went over them as though they were not there. He did not even slow down and it seemed he walked on air across them. I was amazed as I watched him. Maybe it was his momentum that carried him across when he should have fallen through the cracks.

God often uses little things to teach us great lessons in faith and trust. The ant had a built in unconcern. To him obstacles are things to climb over or skip across. The birds do not worry, they just keep on singing and God provides for them. The squirrel does not stop to ponder as he faces a tall tree. He has total trust that God has correctly installed his climbing gear. You never hear the tree complaining. It just stands there holding up its branches like arms as a monument to God.

God tells us in Philippians 4:6-7…”Do not worry about anything….” I read a poem once about two birds talking about humans and how they rush about and worry…It ended by saying something like – “it must be they have no heavenly father such as cares for you and me”.

Fear and faith do not mix. When we fear we are not trusting. David said, “I will trust and not be afraid.” Faith makes the impossible, possible. God reaches out his hand to help us over the cracks and past the obstacles. of life. We need to get our spiritual momentum up to par and practice the trusting in the Lord…and let Him direct our path.

If we only had the faith of an ant we could scurry through life without a worry.
