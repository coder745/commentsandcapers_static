King Solomon

If King Solomon had owned a computer he would have made who’s who on the World Wide Web. He would have needed a full time staff to answer all his email. His advice column would have topped any we have today. His address would have begun with www- standing for wisdom, wealth, and women but sadly his password would have been- Vanity.

He had wisdom given to him by God and was, at the first, a very great king. His accomplishments included the building of a magnificent temple for the worship of god. His wise judgments were known worldwide. People came from far and near to seek his council and advice. He had wealth beyond measure and power to bring peace to Israel during his reign. Everyone knew of his great Wisdom and love for God.

His wisdom began to slip when his wealth and power came between him and his God. There is a great deal of difference between wisdom and being wise. Money and the comforts of life often blind people to their need for God. A luxurious life style began to warp his thinking as he sought the thrill of worldly pleasure. Power and possessions took his heart on a downward path away from God.

He hit a new low when the women in his life began to turn his head. He knew the command of God about being unequally yoked together with unbelievers. He listened to his heart instead of his head and took many unbelieving wives. Wine women and song caused him to trip over his own self centeredness and fall on his face in his own foolishness.

In his hours of regret and despair, depression and guilt, when he uttered his famous words, “Vanity of vanities, all is vanity”, he probably remembered his own words in Proverbs 1:7.”The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge: but fools despise wisdom and instruction.” He had played the fool and lost his influence for God.

If he could send us an Email today, his advice would be clear. Learn from my mistakes. “Fear God and keep his commandment, for this is the whole duty of man. Eccl. 12:13. He also wrote in Proverbs. 4:23, “Keep your heart with all diligence for out of it are the issues of life.”

Today King Solomon would tell us that real and lasting joy comes to our lives only from a right relationship to God, the one who is wisdom. His love is real and is ours for the taking.

In your relationship to God, what is your password?
