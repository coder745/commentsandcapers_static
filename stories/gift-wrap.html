Gift Wrap

As Christmas day gets closer, the packages begin pile up in all their beautiful wrappings. The decorations are in place and parties and meals planned. Wrapping gifts for loved ones is fun and after all,love is what Christmas is all about. I count the gifts to make sure no one is forgotten. It is a feeling of satisfaction when the job is done. Christmas will be a day to remember.

In my growing up years the memories of Christmas wrappings are many and varied. There was the time when a group of college students gave gifts that were beautiful in their simplicity. A group of caring young people who wanted to share. They gathered us as underprivileged children together and provided each of us with new pajamas and slippers wrapped with special care. Something we had never had before. Their caring was the special wrap that I remember best.

Another vivid memory of Christmas wrap was during the days of the great depression. Our hopes at Christmas time were always high. We sang in school about how to behave ourselves so that Santa would bring us toys and things. Times were hard for families as large as ours. We strung popcorn to decorate the tree that came from the woods. We had tinsel but no lights. Still it looked pretty and we went to bed with dreams that Santa would bring us some beautifully wrapped gifts.

Even though we very were poor we believed that Santa had plenty of gifts for everyone. So, very early Christmas morning we awoke and jumped out of bed and ran down the stairs to see the treasures. In our minds we had visions of fun and laughter as we opened our gifts. When we looked under the tree there was absolutely nothing there. No toys, no candy, not anything at all.

Walking back up the stairs was a little slower. Tears were blurring our vision and our hopes were shattered. We did not even notice that our parents had done the best they could. There on each pillow was a tiny gift. It was not wrapped in fancy paper. It was just a very small toy, an orange and two walnuts. The gift wrap was a tiny stocking. Christmas is not wrappings it is loving. Gifts are a reslult of love wrapped or not. It is not really the wrapping or the gift it is the thought that counts.

As I remember finding the tree empty of gifts, I wonder would Jesus would feel if he came to my house on Christmas morning only to find my tree laden with gifts for everyone else but on his birthday absolutely nothing there for him. If he came to my parties and found that there was no place set for him. In all the fun and laughter would he find there were not even any thoughts of Him. Sometimes we get so busy we do not have time to think about what Christmas it is really all about and the reason why we are doing all this.

The gift of God to us on that first Christmas was not beautifully wrapped in fancy paper. It was wrapped in the beauty of love. It was love wrapped in simple fare in a manger in Bethlehem. The love from God was his only son given to pay our sin debt so that we could have the hope of eternal life. How can we give him less than our best?

In all these gifts that required so much time and expense to buy and wrap, have I even had one thought about the one whose birthday we are celebrating? Does he really want a beautifully wrapped gift under the tree? What is the gift he most desires? God once said, My son, give me thine heart. No wrapping required.
