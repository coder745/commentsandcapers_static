Goldenrod

As a child I learned a poem by heart which was a requirement in my grade. I only remember the first verse. I do not remember where the author was leading although I am sure it had a great ending. The first verse went like this:

When the goldenrod is yellow, And the corn is turning brown, And the trees in apple orchards, With their fruits are bending down”…

I believe it goes on to make Goldenrod along with other beauties of fall lovely and desirable. It starts off with giving us a beautiful picture of nature and I thank God for the trees and flowers he sends. He has made all things beautiful. However, beauty does not always mean it is safe to touch or get involved with.

Well, on this particular fall day I was feeling the effects of this golden stuff mentioned. I live in the southern part of the USA where Golden rod and rag weed are in abundance. So, and with apologies to the author whose name at that time was unknown to me, I gave my own version of the above poem about this yellow menace. Here is how it impresses me:

I think this was an author who, Was well known and had clout, But his poem about this wonder, In the South land is in doubt.

When the golden rod is yellow, And its pollen fills the air, You will find its out to get you, You just haven’t got a prayer.

Oh the beauty of the autumn, Gorgeous leaves adored by all, Sweet relief from heat of summer, Till this yellow curse of fall.

First some sneezing then some wheezing, As your nostrils take it in, Down your throat the dripping fluid, Makes you throat as raw as sin.

Itching eyes and head that’s aching, Misery far beyond belief, Sleepless nights and labored breathing, Nothing seems to bring relief.

Coughing just don’t seem to let up, Pass the cough syrup if you please. Kleenex box is getting empty, Please stand back, here comes a sneeze.

When the golden rod is yellow, Think again who has the clout, Cause the pollen is going get you, If you don’t watch out.

Some things that look so pretty can also be irritating and even cause sickness. Satan makes sin look pretty, colorful and inviting too, but if you venture too close and allow it to get you in its clutches, it can be deadly. It will sneak up on you just like pollen and the next thing you know it has you in its grip. There are side effects that we don’t see on the surface. It will leave scars and regrets. Looking good is one of Satan’s tactics.

Harmless looking pleasures are not always as they seem. Jesus told us about that in the story of the Prodigal Son in Luke 15. He had let down his guard and allowed Satan to paint a golden picture of a life that he thought would be everything he wanted. It turned out to be emptiness and disappointment. It caused him worse things than sneezing. It caused him misery beyond belief. God tells us to flee youthful lusts. He also tells us to “Resist the devil and he will flee from you”.

Don’t let the pretty golden appearance of sin fool you. It will do it’s number on you.
